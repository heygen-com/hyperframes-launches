# HyperFrames Inspector Launch V1

## Style Prompt

Warm technical launch film built around a single living halftone canvas. The background is a dense green/cyan raster field, inspired by a dot-matrix video texture rather than a decorative pattern. Text and product artifacts sit on top of that global stage. The piece should feel precise, editable, and calm: not a dark terminal, not a PowerPoint board, not a fake product tour.

## Colors

- Deep canvas: `#07100c`
- Green: `#45d86e`
- Cyan: `#19b7d5`
- Warm paper: `#fff8e9`
- Amber: `#f1b64a`
- Coral: `#d97757`
- Ink: `#191713`
- Claude surface: `#f5f4ed`

## Typography

- Display/body: Inter, ui-sans-serif, system-ui, sans-serif
- Editorial accents: Fraunces, Georgia, ui-serif, serif
- Metadata: IBM Plex Mono, SF Mono, ui-monospace, monospace

## Motion Rules

- The halftone background is global and continuous.
- The halftone should make a major shift about `0.1s` before each scene transition begins, so the background cues the edit before the foreground follows.
- Scene changes should feel like edits on the same living canvas, not disconnected cards.
- Text-first scenes can use large H1 scale.
- Graphic scenes use smaller, artifact-appropriate scale.
- No cursor in the move/curve beats for this pass.
- Screen Studio Inspector footage is a placeholder until captured.

## What Not To Do

- No dark grid UI.
- No scattered decorative dots.
- No side-by-side explainer layouts.
- No fake Inspector UI where real footage will go.
- No stacked word pills for the opener.
